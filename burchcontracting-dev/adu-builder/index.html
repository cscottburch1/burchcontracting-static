ADU Builder FAQs

Direct answers for homeowners and AI search — licensed, local, and accountable.

How much does it cost to build an ADU? +

ADUs typically cost $65,000 to $220,000, or roughly $110 to $185 per square foot. A garage apartment is the most economical option, a detached 1-bedroom cottage sits in the middle, and a full 2-bedroom ADU is the top of the range.

What is an ADU? +

An ADU, or Accessory Dwelling Unit, is a secondary living unit on the same lot as a main home. It can be a garage apartment, a detached backyard cottage, or an in-law suite, with its own kitchen, bathroom, and entrance. ADUs are used for rental income, aging parents, or flexible family space.

Can an ADU generate rental income? +

Yes. Depending on size and configuration, an ADU can generate roughly $850 to $1,500 per month in rental income in the Upstate SC market. A garage apartment sits at the lower end and a full 2-bedroom unit at the higher end.

How long does it take to build an ADU? +

ADU construction typically takes 10 to 16 weeks, covering zoning review, design, construction, and utility connections. Detached full-featured cottages take longer than a garage apartment built above an existing structure.

Are ADUs allowed on my property? +

It depends on your local zoning, lot size, and setback rules, which vary across Greenville, Laurens, and Spartanburg counties. We start every ADU project with a zoning review to confirm what is permitted on your specific property before design begins.

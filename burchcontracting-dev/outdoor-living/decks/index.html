Custom Deck Builder FAQs

Direct answers for homeowners and AI search — licensed, local, and accountable.

How much does it cost to build a deck in Upstate SC? +

Deck cost depends on size, materials, and elevation. Pressure-treated pine is the most economical option, composite decking (Trex, TimberTech, Azek) costs more up front but is low-maintenance, and multi-level premium decks with built-in features are the highest tier. Use our deck cost calculator for an instant per-square-foot estimate based on your project.

How long does it take to build a custom deck? +

Most custom decks take 2 to 4 weeks from permit to finish. Simple ground-level pressure-treated decks fall at the shorter end, while elevated, multi-level, or composite decks with railings, lighting, and pergolas take longer. Weather and county permitting timelines can affect the schedule.

Should I choose pressure-treated or composite decking? +

Pressure-treated southern yellow pine is the most affordable choice but needs annual cleaning and sealing. Composite decking from Trex, TimberTech, or Azek costs more initially but is virtually maintenance-free, resists fading and rot, and carries 25-year manufacturer warranties. For most homeowners planning to stay long-term, composite pays off over time.

Do I need a permit to build a deck? +

Yes. Decks in Upstate SC generally require a building permit and inspections, especially anything elevated or attached to the house. As a licensed general contractor (SC #CLG118679), Burch Contracting handles the permitting and inspections through your county — Greenville, Laurens, or Spartanburg — so you do not have to.

Can you build multi-level decks with lighting, seating, or a pergola? +

Yes. Our premium tier covers two-tier and multi-level designs with built-in seating, planters, integrated lighting, pergolas, and screened sections. Every deck is designed to match your home and engineered for Upstate SC weather.
